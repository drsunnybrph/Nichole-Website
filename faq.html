General

About Madre y Agua and how it works

What is Madre y Agua?

Madre y Agua is a healing practice rooted in ancestral traditions — bringing together herbal medicine, food as medicine, ritual arts, and education. It is not a clinic or a wellness brand. It is a relational body of work designed to help people return to simpler, more intentional ways of caring for themselves.

Who is this work for?

Anyone seeking a deeper connection to their body, their cycles, and the natural world. No prior experience with herbal medicine, ritual, or ancestral practices is needed. This work meets you where you are.

Where is Madre y Agua based?

Sessions are available both in-person and virtually. Reach out to hello@madreyagua.com to discuss what is available in your area.

Is this a replacement for medical care?

No. All offerings at Madre y Agua are complementary to, not a replacement for, conventional medical care. Any health conditions or medications are always discussed as part of the intake process.

How do I know which offering is right for me?

The best place to start is by reading each offering page and noticing what resonates. You can also reach out directly at hello@madreyagua.com — Nichole reads every message personally and can help you find the right fit.

Herbal Medicine

Plant-based consultations and custom formulations

Do I need prior experience with herbs?

Not at all. This work meets you wherever you are — whether you've never touched a medicinal plant or have been working with them for years.

Are consultations in-person or virtual?

Both options are available. Virtual consultations are conducted via video call. Formulations can be shipped or arranged for local pickup depending on your location.

How long until I notice results?

Herbs work with the body's own rhythms. Most people begin to notice shifts within 2–4 weeks, with deeper changes emerging over 1–3 months of consistent practice.

What does an intake consultation include?

A 90-minute session exploring your health history, patterns, constitution, and what your body is asking for — followed by a custom herbal formulation, seasonal protocol, plant guide, and a follow-up session 4–6 weeks later.

Can herbal medicine work alongside my medications?

Yes, but with care. Any medications or existing conditions are discussed thoroughly during intake to ensure the formulation is safe and supportive alongside your current care.

Ancestral Food & Culinary Care

Personal chef services, retreats, and medicinal food

What types of food traditions do you cook from?

The cooking draws primarily from Mesoamerican, Latin American, and indigenous plant-based traditions, while adapting to the specific cultural roots, dietary needs, and seasonal context of each client or event.

Do you accommodate dietary restrictions?

Yes. Every menu is built around your specific needs — including allergies, intolerances, and intentional dietary practices.

How far in advance should I book for an event or retreat?

For retreats and larger events, 4–6 weeks in advance is ideal. For ongoing personal chef arrangements, 2 weeks notice is generally sufficient to begin.

Do you travel for retreats?

Yes. Travel is available for multi-day retreats and extended engagements. Reach out to discuss logistics, availability, and travel arrangements.

Ritual & Feminine Arts

Cyclical living, embodiment, and rites of passage

Do I need to identify as a woman to access this work?

This work is offered with the understanding that feminine energies and cyclical rhythms exist within all bodies. If this work calls to you, you are welcome.

Do I need a regular menstrual cycle to engage with cyclical practices?

No. Cyclical living can be tracked through the lunar cycle for those without a menstrual cycle, and is adapted for those in any phase of life — including perimenopause, menopause, and postpartum.

What does a 1:1 session look like?

Sessions are 75–90 minutes and may include conversation, somatic practices, ritual guidance, and support for wherever you are in your cycle or life. Each session is different depending on what is alive for you.

Can this work be done virtually?

Yes. Much of this work translates beautifully to virtual space. Ceremonies involving physical elements can be adapted through guided practice and material preparation on your end.

Educational Spaces

Workshops, writings, and courses

Do I need prior knowledge to attend a workshop?

No. All workshops are designed to be accessible regardless of prior experience. Beginners are warmly welcomed and the work is rich enough to offer something to those with more background as well.

How can I stay informed about upcoming workshops?

Reach out via email to be added to the mailing list at hello@madreyagua.com. Upcoming offerings are shared there first.

Are workshops offered virtually?

Many workshops are offered both in-person and virtually. The format is specified when each workshop is announced.

Can organizations or schools book private workshops?

Yes. Custom workshops can be arranged for organizations, schools, wellness spaces, and community groups. Reach out to discuss needs, group size, and availability.

Practical Questions

Booking, payment, and logistics

How do I book a session or inquire about an offering?

Email hello@madreyagua.com with a brief description of what you're looking for. Nichole reads every message personally and will respond within a few days.

What is the cancellation policy?

Cancellation details are shared at the time of booking. Generally, 48 hours notice is appreciated for 1:1 sessions. Please reach out directly if circumstances arise.

Are sliding scale rates available?

Accessibility is important to this work. If cost is a barrier, reach out — Nichole holds a limited number of sliding scale spots and is open to conversation.

I don't see my question here. What should I do?

Just ask. Email hello@madreyagua.com with anything that's on your mind — no question is too small or too unusual.
