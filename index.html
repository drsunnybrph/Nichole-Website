— Philosophy

Life is relational.

We are shaped through our relationships — with our bodies, our families, our communities, our ancestors, the land beneath our feet, and the living world that sustains us. Madre y Agua cultivates spaces, practices, and offerings that nourish the relationships that sustain life.

— At Its Heart

Devoted to the ritual arts of living in relationship.

  • A relationship with the body and its rhythms.
  • A relationship with food and the stories it carries.
  • A relationship with beauty, creativity, song, and expression.
  • A relationship with ancestry and inheritance.
  • A relationship with place.
  • A relationship with community.
  • A relationship with the seasons of a life.

Here, cooking becomes a conversation. Song becomes remembrance.
Gathering becomes care. Ritual becomes rhythm.
And the everyday becomes a place where life can be nourished, honored, and carried forward.

— About

Nichole Elana

A first-generation Colombian-American born by the Pacific, where ocean meets memory.

Her roots stretch from the Afro-Colombian rhythms of the Pacific coast to the Andes, the Cherokee lands of the Southeast, and the folk traditions of Swedish-German lineage. She was raised by hands that understood care as ceremony — medicine that lived in flower baths and simmering pots, in remedies whispered in kitchens, in songs carried to the water.

For more than a decade, she has studied herbalism, nourishment, ritual arts, and the many ways people tend life through seasons of change.
