— What This Is

Returning to the body as a site of sacred knowing

Ritual and feminine arts work is a reclamation. It asks: what if your cycle was not a burden, but a teacher? What if the transitions of your life were held as rites of passage rather than moved through alone?

This offering holds space for embodiment, cyclical living, and the re-weaving of ceremony into everyday life — through 1:1 sessions, group containers, and rites of passage work.

This offering includes

  • 1:1 Embodiment & Cycle Sessions
  • Rites of passage ceremonies
  • New & full moon practices
  • Postpartum & menarche support
  • Group ritual containers
— Areas of Work

What This Holds

01

Cyclical Living

Learning to move with the four phases of the menstrual cycle as seasons of inner life — rest, creation, action, and release.

02

Rites of Passage

Ceremonial support for menarche, birth, postpartum, menopause, and other threshold moments that deserve to be witnessed.

03

Embodiment Practice

Somatic and sensory practices that return awareness to the body — breath, movement, touch, and presence as portals.

04

Moon & Seasonal Ritual

Simple, grounded practices for honoring the lunar cycle and the turning of seasons as a living calendar.

05

Group Containers

Circles and seasonal gatherings where women come together to remember, to rest, and to be held in community.

06

Altarmaking & Sacred Space

Creating intentional home altars and sacred spaces that anchor daily practice and honor what matters most.

— Common Questions

Frequently Asked

Do I need to identify as a woman to access this work?

This work is offered with the understanding that feminine energies live within all bodies. If this work calls to you, you are welcome.

Do I need to have a regular cycle to engage with cyclical practices?

No. Cyclical living can be tracked through the lunar cycle for those without a menstrual cycle, and adapted for those in any phase of life.

What does a 1:1 session look like?

Sessions are 75–90 minutes and may include conversation, somatic practices, ritual guidance, and support for wherever you currently are.

Can this work be done virtually?

Yes. Much of this work translates beautifully to virtual space. Ceremonies involving physical elements can be adapted or supported through guided practice.
