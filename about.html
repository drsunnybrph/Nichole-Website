Madre y Agua is not an idea Nichole invented — it is a remembering. A gathering of threads that were always present: in the kitchen of her childhood, in the plants that grew near the places she loved, in the body that asked to be listened to.

The work deepened through years of study, practice, and lived experience — and through the understanding that the most important knowledge does not come only from books, but from relationship. With the land, with the body, with the community of people who have carried this wisdom forward.

— How This Work Is Held

The Guiding Principles

01

Relational, Not Transactional

Every offering is designed to build relationship — between you and your body, between you and the plants, between practitioner and client. Not a service rendered, but a collaboration entered into.

02

Ancestral Without Being Extractive

The traditions this work draws from are honored, credited, and held with humility. The goal is to remember and share — not to commodify or appropriate.

03

Grounded in Lived Experience

This is not theoretical work. Everything offered has been practiced, embodied, and tested against real life — Nichole's own and that of the people she has worked with.

04

Accessible Without Being Watered Down

The work is made accessible not by simplifying what is complex, but by meeting people where they are — without prerequisites, without gatekeeping.
